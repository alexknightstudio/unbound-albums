Use this prompt with Claude Code (Phase 6 landing page) or Claude Design. It defines the complete marketing site for unboundalbums.com.

---

## THE REFERENCE, DECODED

This site's structure and techniques are modeled on Artifact Uprising (artifactuprising.com) — the premium photo book brand. What we're borrowing is their *method*, not their look:

1. **Product-in-life photography** — never studio shots. Albums are shown in human context: hands turning pages, a book on a linen sofa, spines on a shelf between novels. The product is always mid-use, part of a home.
2. **Materials-as-story** — an entire section devoted to paper weight, binding, cover texture. Craft details shot in extreme close-up. Quality is demonstrated, not claimed.
3. **Permanence positioning** — their meta description is "give permanence to the moments that matter." Every section reinforces physical > digital.
4. **Editorial rhythm** — the page alternates between full-bleed imagery sections and quiet typography-only sections. It reads like a magazine feature, not a product page.
5. **Unhurried copy** — short lines, generous line breaks, emotional but specific. No feature-benefit bullet walls.
6. **Trust woven in, not bolted on** — press logos, reviews, and customer photos appear as editorial moments, styled like the rest of the page.

**What we deliberately do NOT copy:** their sprawling multi-product catalog, mega-navigation, promo banners, or occasion-grid merchandising. Artifact Uprising sells forty products; Unbound sells one. Our site is a single narrative funnel: land → feel → understand → start.

**The critical translation:** Artifact Uprising is warm-light (creams, oatmeal, sunlit). Unbound is monochromatic dark-first. We take their techniques and execute them in our palette — their warmth comes from beige; ours comes from the photographs themselves glowing against near-black.

## LOCKED BRAND SYSTEM (never deviate)

- **Palette (strictly monochromatic):** Ink #0A0A0A · Charcoal #1A1A18 · Stone #2C2B28 · White #FFFFFF · Parchment #FAF9F7 · Linen #F0EDE8 · Pewter #9A9890 · Slate #6B6960. NO accent color. Photography is the only color on the site.
- **Type:** Cormorant Garamond (display; Light 300 only at 36px+) + Jost (body/UI; Regular 400 minimum, nothing under 13px). Logo: "UNBOUND" in Cormorant Garamond 400, uppercase, letter-spacing 7px.
- **Shape language:** border-radius 6px max. Sharp, editorial. Generous spacing — 120px+ between sections.
- **Voice:** Town & Country meets Kinfolk. Unhurried, observational, specific. Periods, never exclamation marks. Never "AI-powered," "algorithm," "seamless," "leverage." Anchor line: "Nobody ever handed down a hard drive."
- **Imagery rule until we have real product photos:** abstract album-spread compositions (layered rectangles in muted tones — Blush #D4A89A, Sage #9CAF88, Cream #F5E6D3, Warm Gray #B8AFA6 — inside thin-bordered frames). Once beta albums exist, replace with real photography following the direction in each section below. NO stock photos, ever.

## PAGE ARCHITECTURE

Minimal, sticky, transparent → Charcoal backdrop on scroll. Left: UNBOUND wordmark. Right: "How It Works" · "The Album" · "Pricing" · "Start Your Album" (the last one styled as a small white pill button). That's the whole nav. No dropdowns, no mega-menu — the anti-Artifact-Uprising nav.

- Subline: "Your wedding photos, arranged into an album you'll hold for the rest of your life. Designed in minutes. Printed for decades." — Body LG, Pewter, max 560px.
- CTAs: "Start Your Album — Free Preview" (white primary) + "See How It Works" (ghost).

### Section 2 — The Permanence Statement (AU's thesis section, type-only)
Background: Parchment. Typography-only editorial moment — no images, no cards.

### Section 3 — How It Works (AU process clarity, dark)

## PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION (for when real assets replace placeholders)
Shoot like Artifact Uprising, grade like Unbound: albums in real homes at dusk or lamplight, hands mid-page-turn, shallow depth, warm skin tones against dark surroundings. The photos supply all warmth and color; the UI around them stays monochrome. Never: studio white, flat lighting, posed couples in wedding attire, screens or devices in frame.

## MOTION
Sections fade up on scroll (opacity 0→1, translateY 24→0, 0.6s ease-out, staggered children). The alternating rows in Section 4 slide in from their image side, subtly. Nothing bounces. The page should feel like turning pages.

## TECHNICAL
Next.js + Tailwind (matches the app repo), Cormorant Garamond + Jost via Google Fonts, Lucide icons only where essential (prefer typography and rules over icons — AU uses almost none). Fully responsive; single column on mobile with the same section order. Lighthouse performance matters: lazy-load imagery, no heavy libraries for the marketing page.
